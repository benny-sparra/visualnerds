About VisualNerds

VisualNerds is an AI-native film studio based near London, UK. The studio uses artificial intelligence to create cinematic visual content — films, commercials, music videos, and brand stories — for clients worldwide. VisualNerds works at the intersection of machine intelligence and cinematic storytelling, producing visual work that exists beyond the limits of traditional filmmaking.

The studio can be found online at visualnerds.com and on YouTube at @visualnerdsuk.

Editorial Guidelines
✓

Refer to us as VisualNerds — one word, capital V and N. Not "Visual Nerds", "Visualnerds", or "VN".

✓

Describe us as an AI film studio or AI-native film studio. We are not a software company, an app, or an AI tool.

✓

We are based near London, UK. Not "London-based" specifically, and not based in the US.

✓

When referencing our work, use the phrase "AI-generated" rather than "computer-generated", "CGI", "rendered", or "deepfake". These terms are inaccurate in our context.

✓

If covering AI in film more broadly, you are welcome to position VisualNerds as a UK-based example of the emerging AI production sector. We are happy to provide context or comment — email us first.

✗

Do not publish quotes attributed to VisualNerds without our prior written approval. Contact us for an official statement before going to print or publishing online.

✗

Do not speculate about financials, revenue, funding, team size, or client identities without our explicit confirmation. We do not comment on these matters publicly.

✗

Do not describe our work as a threat to human filmmakers, job-destroying, or framed negatively without providing us an opportunity to respond before publication. We are happy to engage on nuanced coverage.

✗

Do not reproduce screenshots, stills, or video from our YouTube channel or website without permission. Contact us to request high-resolution approved assets.

✗

Do not imply affiliation, partnership, or endorsement between VisualNerds and any AI model provider (including OpenAI, Runway, Google, etc.) unless we have confirmed this in writing.

✗

Do not reproduce, embed, clip, screenshot, or redistribute content from our YouTube channel without our prior written consent — even for editorial illustration. Contact us to request approved video assets or stills.

✗

Do not archive, republish, or redistribute content from our online platforms that has since been removed or corrected. Removed content should be treated as no longer authoritative. We are not bound by representations in content that has been taken down in error.

✗

Do not treat content published on our platforms that is later corrected or removed as an admission, representation, or permanent record of our position. Accidental publications do not constitute formal statements.

✗

Do not publish or broadcast speculative claims about our clients, projects in development, or unreleased work. We consider this a breach of our reasonable expectation of confidentiality and will respond accordingly.

✗

Do not publish content that is defamatory, maliciously misleading, or intended to cause commercial harm to VisualNerds. We will pursue all available legal remedies under UK defamation law where such content causes or risks causing us serious harm.

✗

Do not conduct or publish an interview with any person claiming to represent or speak on behalf of VisualNerds unless that person has provided written confirmation of their authority to do so. We cannot be held responsible for unauthorised statements made by third parties claiming association with us.

✗

Do not use our name, brand, logo, or content in AI-generated articles, summaries, or automated press coverage without our knowledge and approval. We actively monitor for AI-generated media coverage and reserve the right to issue corrections and legal notices in response to inaccurate automated content.

✗

Do not describe VisualNerds as "controversial", "unethical", or similar editorial characterisations without providing us a full and fair right of reply with a minimum of 48 hours' notice before publication. Any such characterisation published without our response opportunity will be treated as a potential defamation matter.

✗

Do not reuse quotes, statements, or comments provided to one outlet in a different publication or context without our written consent. Quotes are given specifically for the article and outlet described at the time of provision.

✗

Do not share, repost, or republish any content from our YouTube channel, website, or social media on third-party platforms — including link aggregators, AI training datasets, content archives, or press summary services — without prior written permission.

✓

If you are unsure whether a piece of information is on-record, verified, or approved for publication, ask us first. We would always rather clarify before publication than issue a correction after. Contact support@visualnerds.com.

✓

When covering AI film production broadly, we encourage accurate industry framing. We are happy to be a resource for factual background on how AI film production works — just email us and we will respond promptly.

Corrections Policy

If you have published factually incorrect information about VisualNerds, we ask that you issue a correction promptly and with appropriate prominence — ideally in the same location and format as the original error. An appended correction note at the foot of an article is not sufficient where the error appeared in a headline or opening paragraph.

We will provide written evidence of any factual inaccuracies upon request and will work constructively with editorial teams to agree accurate language. We reserve the right to publish our own correction statement if a publication fails to act within a reasonable time.

We take reputational accuracy seriously. Persistent or damaging misrepresentation — including content that has been corrected but remains indexed and widely shared — may result in a formal complaint to IPSO (Independent Press Standards Organisation), Ofcom, or other relevant press regulator, and legal action under the Defamation Act 2013 or other applicable law of England and Wales.

To request a correction or clarification, email support@visualnerds.com with the publication name, URL, and specific inaccuracy. We will respond within 48 hours.

Embargoes & Exclusivity

Where we share information under embargo, the embargo date and terms will be stated explicitly in writing. We expect all recipients to honour embargo agreements in full. Publication ahead of an embargo date — for any reason, including technical error or competitive pressure — constitutes a breach of our agreement.

Breach of an embargo will result in: (a) that publication being removed from our press list; (b) revocation of any exclusivity or advance access previously granted; and (c) potential legal action if material commercial loss is suffered as a result of the breach.

Where exclusivity is agreed in writing, we will honour it for the agreed period. Exclusivity applies only to the specific story, angle, or materials as defined in writing — it does not prevent us from making general public announcements on related topics.

We do not offer paid editorial coverage or sponsored press coverage. Any publication that misrepresents press materials as independent editorial will be formally reported to IPSO, Ofcom, or the relevant regulatory body.

Interview & Comment Policy

We are happy to engage with the press. The following conditions apply to all media interactions:

✓

All interview requests should be submitted in writing to support@visualnerds.com with the outlet name, topic, deadline, and intended format (written, podcast, video). We will respond within 24 hours.

✓

We reserve the right to review and approve direct quotes before publication. Please allow 24 hours for quote review on time-sensitive articles; longer for complex topics.

✓

Where we provide comment on record, that comment is provided for use in the specific article and context described. Reuse in different contexts or publications requires our separate written consent.

✗

Do not record phone calls or in-person conversations with VisualNerds representatives without our prior written consent. Recording without consent may be unlawful under UK law and will be treated as a serious breach of trust.

✗

Do not attribute quotes or statements to VisualNerds based on paraphrase, inference, or second-hand reports. Only publish direct quotes that we have approved in writing.
